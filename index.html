What is a case manager?

To start with the life of a foster child you need to first understand what is means to be a Foster Care Case Manager You might ask,“What is case managing?” Case managing consist typically of some type of demographic that you are doing front line work in order to help or aid someone through a situation where that be for a short or long term. I have worked with Domestic violence victims, Homeless teenagers, mentally ill homeless singles and families, teenage girls and now foster care. All of these groups have something in common. They are all demographic that has lacked a way to advocate for themselves. In these situations you have the people not feeling heard and their counterparts(parents,courts and etc) not feeling as though the ones in need are effectively hearing them.

Once a child(ren) is/are brought into care

An appropriate home(foster,relative or kinship) is located where the child can go to while more information is gathered. Once the child is in that home the adult has 72 hours to have the child seen by a physician to determine if there are any health issues which could include bruises,broken bones and etc. that can be indicative of physical abuse. During this time the case is assigned to a Foster Care Case Manager. Within 72 business hours the matter is brought before a Commissioner or Judge as a Protective Custody Hearing(PC). At this hearing, is it determined if the reasons why the are brought into care child(ren) brought into care are still present. If they are not then the child(ren )are allowed to return home. If those concerns are still valid then the child(ren) will remain in care . During this hearing the child(ren) is appointed an attorney as well as the parents can apply for a free attorney( determined by their monthly income and family size).

Adjudication

This is when all the information concerning the allegations are brought before the Judge/Commissioner to determien if the reasons this child came into care are valid.

There are 3 possible options that can happen:

  1. The allegations where not unsubstantiated/not sustained
    • The allegations that brought the case in care were NOT proved with clear and convincing evidence which results in the children going home
  2. The case is substantiated/sustained
    • The allegations that brought the case in care was proved with clear and convincing proof.
  3. The case ended in stipulation
    • The parents admit they did someting wrong and the case can be proved as such they forgo a trial

In the last two options the case moves to dispostion while the 1st option ends the case(in some situations)

  • The dispostion is when the case manager would testify as to what has occured since the child(ren)has come into care to the date of the Adjudication.

Continous

After the disposition, court is held every 3-6 months to determine if the same issues that brought the child(ren) into care are still present. Depending on the concerns and resolve of the parents directly correlate with the length of the case. Eventually, if a case continues on without hope of reunifying it is then determine if there is another permanent option (adoption or guardianship) that would be in the best interest of the children. Cases can last for 6 months to years upon years. There are numerous providers that give their professional opinions but, it the final decesion on everything is up to the Judge or Commissioner.
